# Research Planning

A systematic approach to research planning to ensure smooth project execution.

## 1. Timeline Planning

### 1.1 Research Phase Structure

**Typical research cycle** (6-month example):

| Phase | Duration | Main Tasks | Deliverables |
|-------|----------|-----------|--------------|
| **Phase 1: Preparation** | Month 1 | Literature review, problem definition | Literature survey, research proposal |
| **Phase 2: Exploration** | Months 2-3 | Preliminary experiments, method validation | Proof of concept, initial results |
| **Phase 3: Development** | Months 4-5 | Method refinement, comprehensive experiments | Complete experimental results |
| **Phase 4: Completion** | Month 6 | Paper writing, code cleanup | Paper draft, open-source code |

### 1.2 Time Allocation Principles

**80/20 Rule**:
- 80% of time on core work (experiments, analysis, writing)
- 20% of time on support work (literature, tools, communication)

**Buffer time**:
- Reserve 20% buffer time per phase
- To handle unexpected issues and delays

**Parallel tasks**:
- Literature review spans the entire process
- Paper writing can start early (Introduction, Related Work)
- Code cleanup proceeds in parallel with experiments

## 2. Milestone Definitions

### 2.1 Key Milestones

**Milestone 1: Research Proposal Complete**
- Timing: End of Month 1
- Criteria:
  - Literature review complete (20-30 core papers)
  - Research question clearly defined
  - Methodology direction established
  - Experiment plan formulated

**Milestone 2: Proof of Concept**
- Timing: End of Month 3
- Criteria:
  - Initial experiments complete
  - Method feasibility validated
  - At least one positive result
  - Major challenges identified

**Milestone 3: Full Experiments**
- Timing: End of Month 5
- Criteria:
  - All experiments complete
  - Results meet expectations
  - Ablation studies complete
  - Visualization and analysis complete

**Milestone 4: Paper Submission**
- Timing: End of Month 6
- Criteria:
  - Paper draft complete
  - Code ready for open-sourcing
  - Internal review passed
  - Ready to submit

### 2.2 Checkpoint Setup

**Weekly checks**:
- Progress review
- Issue identification
- Plan adjustment

**Monthly reviews**:
- Milestone assessment
- Risk evaluation
- Resource adjustment

## 3. Risk Identification and Mitigation

### 3.1 Common Risks

**Technical risks**:
- Method does not work
- Experimental results are unsatisfactory
- Insufficient computational resources
- Technical difficulty exceeds expectations

**Mitigation strategies**:
- Prepare fallback approaches
- Early proof of concept
- Request additional resources
- Seek technical support

**Schedule risks**:
- Progress delays
- Dependency delays
- Unexpected events

**Mitigation strategies**:
- Reserve buffer time
- Parallel tasks
- Priority management
- Adjust plans promptly

**Resource risks**:
- GPU unavailability
- Dataset issues
- Team changes

**Mitigation strategies**:
- Multiple resource channels
- Backup datasets
- Document knowledge thoroughly

### 3.2 Risk Monitoring

**Red alert** (address immediately):
- Core method failure
- Critical resource missing
- Severe schedule delay

**Yellow alert** (monitor closely):
- Some experiments unsatisfactory
- Resource constraints
- Minor delays

**Green status** (proceed normally):
- On schedule
- Resources sufficient
- Results meet expectations

## 4. Resource Allocation

### 4.1 Compute Resources

**GPU allocation**:
- Exploration phase: 1-2 GPUs
- Development phase: 2-4 GPUs
- Completion phase: 4-8 GPUs (large-scale experiments)

**Storage allocation**:
- Datasets: 100-500 GB
- Model checkpoints: 50-200 GB
- Experiment logs: 10-50 GB

### 4.2 Personnel Allocation

**Solo project**:
- Literature review: 20%
- Experiment development: 50%
- Paper writing: 20%
- Other: 10%

**Team project**:
- Research lead: Planning, guidance, writing
- Research assistant: Experiments, analysis, coding
- Collaborators: Specific modules, review

### 4.3 Time Allocation

**Weekly time** (40 hours):
- Core research: 25-30 hours
- Literature reading: 5-8 hours
- Meetings and discussions: 3-5 hours
- Other tasks: 2-5 hours

**Daily time**:
- Deep work: 4-6 hours (morning)
- Experiment monitoring: 2-3 hours
- Literature and writing: 1-2 hours
- Communication and collaboration: 1 hour
