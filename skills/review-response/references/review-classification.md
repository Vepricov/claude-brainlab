# Review Comment Classification Standards

This document provides a systematic approach to classifying review comments, helping quickly identify and prioritize different types of comments.

## Classification System

### 1. Major Issues

**Definition**: Issues involving core aspects of the research method, experimental design, result interpretation, etc., requiring substantial revision or additional experiments.

**Characteristics**:
- Questions the validity of the research method
- Requests key experiments or comparisons
- Points out logical problems in result interpretation
- Questions the innovation or contribution of the research

**Keyword identification**:
- "major concern"
- "fundamental issue"
- "missing experiments"
- "insufficient evidence"
- "not convincing"

**Example**:
> "The authors should compare their method with recent work [X]. Without this comparison, it's difficult to assess the true contribution."

**Suggested response strategy**: Accept + Experiment (accept the comment and conduct additional experiments)

---

### 2. Minor Issues

**Definition**: Issues requiring clarification, additional explanation, or small revisions that do not affect the core contribution of the research.

**Characteristics**:
- Requests clarification on certain details
- Suggests improving wording or organization
- Requests additional related work discussion
- Points out that figures or tables could be improved

**Keyword identification**:
- "minor concern"
- "could be improved"
- "please clarify"
- "it would be better"
- "suggestion"

**Example**:
> "The notation in Section 3.2 is confusing. Please clarify the meaning of variable α."

**Suggested response strategy**: Accept + Clarify (accept the comment and provide clarification)

---

### 3. Typos/Formatting

**Definition**: Surface-level issues such as spelling errors, grammatical issues, and formatting inconsistencies.

**Characteristics**:
- Points out spelling errors
- Notes grammatical issues
- Points out inconsistent citation formatting
- Figure or table label errors

**Keyword identification**:
- "typo"
- "grammar"
- "formatting"
- "inconsistent"
- "Figure X should be"

**Example**:
> "Page 5, line 3: 'recieve' should be 'receive'."

**Suggested response strategy**: Accept (accept directly and correct)

---

### 4. Misunderstandings

**Definition**: Reviewers' misunderstandings or misreadings of the paper content, requiring polite clarification.

**Characteristics**:
- Reviewer misunderstood the method or results
- Reviewer overlooked existing explanations in the paper
- Reviewer has an incorrect understanding of a concept

**Keyword identification**:
- "The authors did not..." (but they actually did)
- "It is unclear..." (but the paper already explains it)
- "Why don't the authors..." (but they already did)

**Example**:
> "The authors did not compare with baseline X."
> (But Table 2 in the paper already includes this comparison)

**Suggested response strategy**: Clarify (politely point to existing content in the paper)

---

## Usage Guide

### Classification Process

1. **First pass** - Quickly scan all review comments, mark keywords
2. **Label classification** - Label each comment by type (Major/Minor/Typo/Misunderstanding)
3. **Priority sorting** - Sort by importance (Major > Misunderstanding > Minor > Typo)
4. **Develop strategies** - Formulate a response strategy for each comment

### Priority Recommendations

**High priority** (must handle first):
- Major Issues - require additional experiments or major revisions
- Misunderstandings - need clarification to prevent misunderstanding from deepening

**Medium priority**:
- Minor Issues - need improvement but don't affect core contribution

**Low priority**:
- Typos/Formatting - correct directly

### Notes

- **Don't ignore any comment** - Even a small typo should be acknowledged in the rebuttal
- **Handle misunderstandings politely** - Use "We apologize for the confusion" rather than "The reviewer misunderstood"
- **Major Issues require substantive responses** - Cannot just clarify; require action (experiments, revisions, etc.)
